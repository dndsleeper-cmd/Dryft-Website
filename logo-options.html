Logo concepts

Five takes on the Dryft mark.

All five are D-based, single-color, bold geometric. Each interprets the “car drift / skid mark” brief differently. Open this file in your browser and pick the one you want me to ship — I’ll then apply it everywhere and produce the favicon variants.

Option 01 — Twin Track

The two-tire skid.

Two parallel curved strokes form the D. Reads as a letter, but the construction language is unmistakably two tire tracks curving through a turn. Bold rounded ends emphasize the “planted into pavement” feel. Strong at small sizes.

Option 02 — Trailing D

D, mid-motion.

A confident solid D with two small dots trailing off behind it — the visual wake of something that was just here and is still moving. Subtle drift cue. Letter is unmistakable; the dots add the brand identity without dominating.

Option 03 — Echo

Motion-blur D.

Two D shapes offset diagonally, the back one ghosted to ~30% opacity. Reads as a double-print — a stamp made while moving. Most editorial of the five; would carry well across motion, video, and app icons.

Option 04 — Stencil

The painted-on D.

A single thick outlined D — no fill, just a heavy stroke. Reads as a stencil mark, or paint applied with a fat brush. The most “street-ready” of the five — confident, mature, prints clean at any size, and the negative space inside gives the mark room to breathe.

Option 05 — Apex

Spine + sweep.

A solid vertical spine paired with a single fat swept curve — like the inside line versus the outside line through a corner. Two clearly different gestures: the planted (the rectangle) and the sweeping (the curve). Aggressive without being loud.
