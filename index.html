The Problem

Dashboards tell you what happened.
Not what to do next.

You've seen the charts. Your spending is $400 over in "dining." Great. Now what? Most apps stop there — leaving you with guilt but no guidance.

Notifications fire too late.
After the damage is done.

An "over budget" notification at the end of the month is a post-mortem, not accountability. The money is already gone. You needed the warning on day 8.

Budgets break by the second week.

You set a $300 grocery budget with the best intentions. By the 10th, it's gone. The app doesn't care. It just waits until next month to let you fail again.

Goals are disconnected from daily life.

"Save $5,000 this year" lives in a spreadsheet. Your $7 coffee lives in the real world. Nothing connects the two — until you're three months behind and wondering why.

"The problem isn't the big purchases. It's the hundred small ones you never noticed — until they added up to a month's rent."

— What Dryft is built to prevent

How It Works

Built for the moments you're most likely to dryft

Most finance apps are built around dashboards. Dryft is built around accountability — in real time, before habits become expensive.

01 — Connect

Link Your Banks

02 — Plan

AI Builds Your Plan

03 — Stay Sharp

Life changes. Plans should too. Ask Dryft to adjust your budget conversationally. When a purchase can't be categorized, Dryft asks — you answer once.
