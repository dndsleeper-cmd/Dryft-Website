Logo concepts · round 2

Five more, deeper into the drift.

This batch leans harder into the skid-mark / racing-line language. Some are still D-based, others abandon the letter entirely and brand on the curve. All are original, single-color, and work at any size. Reply with the option number — e.g. "go with 7" — and I'll ship it.

Option 06 — Arc-Cut

D with a skid through it.

A solid D with a curving skid mark cut clean through the bowl. The D is the brand letter; the curve is what Dryft does. Two ideas, one mark. Strong negative-space play — reads as a D first, then you notice the cut.

Option 07 — Apex

The racing line.

No letter. Just a single bold curve that traces the apex of a turn — the optimal line through a corner. Most abstract option of the bunch; relies entirely on the wordmark beside it for naming. Brand-defining, but takes longer to "earn" the D association.

Option 08 — Negative

The D is the cutout.

A solid rounded square (think app icon) with a precise D cut out of it. The letter is the absence, not the form. Reads instantly as an app icon — already pre-shaped to live on a home screen. Confident, modern, very fintech-2025.

Option 09 — Velocity

D in motion.

A solid D pulling forward, three short parallel speed lines trailing in its wake (in front of the letter, so it reads as direction of travel). Manga-influenced. Reads as fast, kinetic, decisive — without being literal about cars or tires.

Option 10 — One-Stroke

Drawn in one go.

A D that looks hand-drawn but is precise — single continuous heavy stroke, rounded ends, no fill. Feels like a confident gesture. Carries a hint of the "first marks on pavement" energy without being literal. The most editorial of these five.
